Why You'll Fall In Love With Panamá City

Panamá City is hands-down the most cosmopolitan capital in Central America. As a thriving center for international banking and trade, Panamá City has a beautifully constructed and expansive skyline. There are more than 110 high-rise projects under construction and 127 already built, making the city #45 in the world-ranking count of high-rise buildings. Ever on the rise, here are just a few of the things you'll love about Panamá City.

Panamá Canal

There is no way you can miss seeing the 8th wonder of the world during your stay in Panamá. Coming in at about fifty miles long, the interoceanic Panamá Canal is the country's biggest attraction - literally. You can hop on a transit tour and share water with an 80,000 ton cargo freighter or check out the engineering marvel from about a half dozen spots in or around the capital like the Calzada de Amador and the Balboa Yacht Club, but the best views are from the four-story Miraflores Lock Visitor Center.

A city of tomorrow

As one of the only three beta world cities in Central America, Panamá City boosts a first-world infrastructure and is the second largest Free Trade Zone in the world. With a rapidly growing economy, the city has a sultry skyline of shimmering glass and steel towers, a banking sector with 90 banks, tap water you can drink, world-class hotels and restaurants and the best shopping in Latin America.

Casco Viejo

Like most metropolitans, Panamá City is divided into boroughs or corregimientos and one of the most popular is Casco Viejo. Spanish for old quarter, Casco Viejo is the historic district of the city and the orange-tiled roofs and Spanish colonial architecture are a welcomed contrast to the skyscraper skyline across the bay.

Calzada De Amador

Calzada de Amador, or Amador Causeway, stretches two miles into the Pacific and connects three islands to the mainland. Calzada de Amador has panoramic views of the city's skyline, the canal's Pacific entrance, and the Bay of Panamá, and a good selection of restaurants and bars that are cooled by ocean breezes. Calzada de Amador is also the gateway to The Day After's venue Figali Plaza and Convention Center.

Art, Museums, And Architecture

One of the first things you'll notice when you explore Panamá City is that the city is rich in art of many forms and museums that cultivate the country's history and customs. Architecturally, Panamá City has many styles with its old quarter buildings ranging from Spanish colonial style to French and Antillean townhouses. You can view most of the city's aesthetics just by walking on foot, but if you want to delve deeper, there are many museums you can check out. Or favorite is the Museo de Arte Contemporáneo, located in Panamá City.
